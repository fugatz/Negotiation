## Negotiation Engine Current State

Repository:

- `/Users/gibbin/Desktop/projects/Negotiation`

Current role:

- Standalone Python dry-run simulator.
- Not production pricing logic yet.
- Runs late in the workflow: after upstream matching, before talent is presented to the client.

Core flow:

```text
Structured project and matched candidate inputs
  -> late-stage creative/practical/pricing scoring
  -> pricing intelligence
  -> timing horizon nudge
  -> behavior / reliability nudge
  -> admin-only AI pricing rationale
  -> call-for-details or email-offer outreach with proposed project rate
  -> talent accept/decline/counter before client presentation
  -> curated slate using locked talent-approved rates
  -> client decision simulation
  -> actualization and outcome learning
```

Key invariants:

- Talent-owned rate ranges remain the pricing authority.
- This engine does not parse messy briefs; brief extraction already exists upstream.
- This engine does not own `clientTrustScore` or `clientTrustTier`; it consumes them from the main site.
- This engine can consume `projectCredibilityScore` when supplied.
- Client trust and brand desirability should remain separate concepts.
- Country market-cost priors are admin-only advisory context until paid-rate actuals exist.
- Paid-rate actuals can override country market-cost priors as advisory market evidence.
- Published actor rate cards can override country market-cost priors for applicable markets/roles.
- Actor recommendations include a bounded internal talent-advocacy uplift because Distinkt represents
  talent and tries to improve the outcome when booking realism allows.
- Admin inclusion override is curation-only, not a rate override.
- Admin overrides cannot bypass hard eligibility, legal floors, or talent acceptance.
- Talent must see the project-specific rate during call-for-details or email-offer outreach.
- Once presented to the client, the quote is locked unless client-side facts change.
- Brand-facing rationales stay positive and non-pricing.
- Talent-facing job-specific pricing rationales are not generated by this layer.
- Admin-facing rationales may explain pricing math, behavior nudges, timing nudges, and discretion.

## Review Prompt

```text

Review the proposed Negotiation pricing/deal-intelligence simulator against the existing main-site
talent matching flow, client trust score, project credibility score, and curator review logic.

Your job is not to rewrite the simulator. Your job is to identify integration risks, missing fields,
prompt mismatches, and product boundary issues before this pricing engine moves toward shadow-mode
integration.

IMPORTANT BOUNDARIES:
- Do not suggest duplicating brief extraction inside the pricing engine.
- Do not suggest exposing hidden floors, negative behavior signals, or pricing math to talent or brands.
- Do not suggest that AI override talent-owned rates.
```
